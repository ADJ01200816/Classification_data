The patient's medical records include details such as the date of the last outpatient visit, the emergency department admission date, the dates of various examinations and tests conducted, the prescribed medications with dosages, the course of treatment followed, the surgical procedures performed, the anesthesia details, the records of blood transfusions if any, the nursing care provided, the admission and discharge dates, the discharge summary, and any referral information to other hospitals. The patient's contact information is also documented, including their phone number. The financial aspect of the patient's healthcare is reflected in the amount paid for services, the insurance coverage details, and the out-of-pocket expenses. The patient's demographic information includes their date of birth, marital status, and the address where they were treated. Additionally, the patient's personal identifiers such as their Social Security number or a unique patient identifier are recorded for administrative purposes.
