His current medical history includes hypertension, managed with Lisinopril 20 mg daily, and type 2 diabetes, controlled through Metformin 500 mg twice daily. His past medical history reveals a myocardial infarction in 2018, followed by a coronary artery bypass grafting procedure. His family history is significant for his father's early onset of coronary artery disease at age 55 and his mother's diagnosis of breast cancer at age 60.

Symptoms reported include occasional dizziness, shortness of breath during exertion, and nocturnal leg cramps. Recent test data indicated a fasting blood glucose level of 7.2 mmol/L, HbA1c of 6.9%, and LDL cholesterol at 3.5 mmol/L. Lifestyle factors include a sedentary job as a software engineer, a diet high in processed foods, and a smoking history of 20 pack-years, having quit 5 years ago. Gene sequencing results identified a variant in the APOE gene associated with increased cardiovascular risk.
