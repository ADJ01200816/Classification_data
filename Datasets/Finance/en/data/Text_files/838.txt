Bank A, headquartered in New York, reported a quarterly profit of $1.2 billion, reflecting a 5% increase from the previous quarter. The institution's risk exposure, as measured by the Value at Risk (VaR) metric, stood at $150 million, within the acceptable threshold of $100 million to $200 million set by regulatory standards. The bank's capital adequacy ratio was recorded at 12.5%, surpassing the minimum requirement of 10% mandated by the Basel III framework. Liquidity coverage ratio (LCR) for the same period was 125%, well above the regulatory minimum of 100%, indicating a robust ability to meet short-term obligations. Key related indicators, such as the non-performing loan ratio, were maintained at 2.3%, below the target value of 3%. The cost-to-income ratio was 45%, falling within the target range of 40% to 50%. Additionally, the bank's return on equity (ROE) was 11.5%, aligning with the strategic target of 10% to 12%. These figures were disclosed in the latest financial report, providing stakeholders with a comprehensive overview of the bank's financial health and operational efficiency.
