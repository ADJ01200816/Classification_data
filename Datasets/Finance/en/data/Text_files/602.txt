The Office for Financial Stability Oversight Council, a division within the United States Department of Treasury, conducted a thorough review on June 15, 2023, focusing on the stability metrics of JPMorgan Chase & Co. This entity, headquartered at 383 Madison Avenue, New York, NY, operates under the jurisdiction of the U.S. regulatory framework. The assessment included an in-depth analysis of the bank's asset portfolio, which as of Q1 2023, totaled $3.4 trillion. Key indicators such as leverage ratio stood at 6.5%, surpassing the minimum requirement of 3% as stipulated by the Basel III accords.

The Federal Reserve System, utilizing data extracted from the Consolidated Financial Statements, evaluated the liquidity coverage ratio (LCR) of the institution, recorded at 120%, well above the mandatory 100%. Additionally, the evaluation considered the bank's net stable funding ratio (NSFR), which was documented at 1.15, reflecting a robust funding profile. The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau provided transactional data revealing that consumer loans amounted to $450 billion, with a non-performing loan ratio of 1.2%.

The Securities and Exchange Commission reviewed JPMorgan's capital adequacy, noting a Tier 1 capital ratio of 14.3%. The Office of the Comptroller of the Currency scrutinized derivative exposures, reporting a notional value of $50 trillion, with a gross credit exposure of $200 billion. The Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation assessed deposit insurance coverage, indicating that 98% of deposits were insured, amounting to $1.2 trillion out of total deposits of $1.25 trillion.

Compliance with the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act was verified through stress tests administered by the Federal Reserve, where JPMorgan demonstrated resilience under adverse economic scenarios, maintaining a minimum Tier 1 common equity ratio of 9.5%. The evaluation also encompassed cybersecurity protocols, with the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority reporting zero significant breaches in the past fiscal year. 

The Internal Revenue Service provided data on tax contributions, with JPMorgan's corporate tax liability for FY2022 amounting to $8.5 billion, reflecting an effective tax rate of 21%. The National Credit Union Administration cross-referenced credit union exposures, which were minimal, totaling $5 billion. The Commodity Futures Trading Commission reviewed the bank's participation in derivatives markets, noting a daily trading volume of $1 trillion in interest rate swaps.

This comprehensive evaluation, utilizing granular financial data and regulatory inputs, facilitated a detailed understanding of JPMorgan Chase & Co.'s systemic importance and operational resilience within the financial ecosystem.
