It appears that you've listed various pieces of information that are typically associated with an individual's educational background. In a structured format like an academic record or an education profile, these details would be organized as follows:

- **Personal Information**: This includes but is not limited to the full name, date of birth, contact information, and identification numbers of the individual.

- **Education Status**: This indicates the current standing of the individual in their educational pursuits. It could be "enrolled," "on leave," "graduated," "withdrawn," etc.

- **Recorded Data**: This is a broad term that could refer to any data points recorded about the student, including grades, attendance records, disciplinary actions, or academic progress.

- **Enrollment Date**: The date on which the individual officially became a student at a particular institution.

- **Graduation Date**: The date on which the individual successfully completed their degree requirements, if applicable.

- **School Name**: The name of the educational institution where the individual is or was enrolled.

- **School Department**: The specific department, faculty, or college within the school where the student's major or field of study is housed.

- **Education Level**: The level of education, such as "primary," "secondary," "undergraduate," "graduate," or "postgraduate."

- **Degree**: The type of degree the student is pursuing or has obtained, such as Bachelor of Arts (BA), Master of Science (MS), Doctor of Philosophy (PhD), etc.

- **Subject Information**: Details about the specific subjects, courses, or major area of study that the student is or was involved in.

For privacy reasons, it's important to handle such information with care and to comply with relevant data protection laws, such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in the European Union or the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) in the United States. Only authorized individuals and institutions should have access to this data, and it should only be used for legitimate purposes.
