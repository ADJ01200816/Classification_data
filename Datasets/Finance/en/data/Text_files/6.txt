It looks like you're listing various pieces of information that could be associated with an individual's employment record. Here's a brief explanation of each term:

1. Individual: The specific person whose employment details are being described.

2. Work Unit: The department, team, or section within the organization where the individual is employed or assigned to work.

3. Work Start Date: The date when the individual began their employment with the company or started in their current position.

4. Work End Date: The date when the individual's employment with the company ended, if applicable. This could be due to resignation, termination, retirement, or the completion of a contract.

5. Position: The specific job title or role that the individual holds within the organization.

6. Work Location: The physical address or geographic location where the individual performs their job duties, which could be an office, a worksite, or remotely from home.

7. Income Status: This could refer to the type of income the individual receives (e.g., full-time salary, part-time hourly wage, contract work, etc.).

8. Income Value: The amount of money the individual earns, often specified on an annual, monthly, weekly, or hourly basis, corresponding to their income status.

These details are commonly found in employment records, job descriptions, resumes, or other documents used for human resources management and financial reporting.
