The Bank of New York Mellon Corp reported a quarterly net income of $1.5 billion, reflecting a 12% increase from the previous year. The institution's Tier 1 capital ratio stood at 14.2%, well above the regulatory minimum of 6%. Liquidity coverage ratio (LCR) was recorded at 120%, indicating a robust ability to meet short-term obligations. The non-performing loan ratio was 1.3%, lower than the industry average of 2.0%. The return on equity (ROE) reached 11.5%, surpassing the target value of 10%. The bank's operational risk loss was $50 million, within the expected range of $40-60 million. Net interest margin (NIM) was 1.8%, falling slightly short of the target range of 1.9-2.1%. Customer deposit growth was 5% year-over-year, aligning with the projected growth rate of 4-6%. The cost-to-income ratio was 55%, improved from the previous year's 58%, nearing the target of 50%. The bank's total assets were $2.3 trillion, with a loan-to-deposit ratio of 80%, maintaining a balanced asset-liability structure.
