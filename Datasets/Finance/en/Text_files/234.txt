Bank A, headquartered in New York, reported a quarterly profit of $1.2 billion, reflecting a 5% increase from the previous quarter. The institution's risk exposure, measured by the Value at Risk (VaR) metric, stood at $50 million, within the acceptable threshold of $30 million to $70 million. The bank's capital adequacy ratio was recorded at 12.5%, surpassing the regulatory minimum of 10%. Liquidity coverage ratio (LCR) was 115%, well above the required 100%, indicating sufficient high-quality liquid assets to cover net cash outflows over a 30-day stress period. The net interest margin (NIM), a key profitability indicator, was 3.2%, falling within the target range of 2.8% to 3.5%. Non-performing loans (NPL) ratio was 1.8%, below the target ceiling of 2.0%. The cost-to-income ratio was 55%, aligning with the strategic goal of maintaining it below 60%. Customer deposit growth was 8% year-over-year, meeting the annual growth target of 7% to 9%. The return on equity (ROE) was 14%, comfortably within the desired band of 12% to 16%. These metrics collectively demonstrated the bank's robust financial health and effective risk management practices.
