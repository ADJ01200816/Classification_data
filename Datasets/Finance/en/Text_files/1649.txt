On the liability side, $10,000,000 was accounted for by short-term debts due within the next fiscal year, and the other $10,000,000 represented long-term liabilities, including bonds and mortgages. The financial health of the company was further underscored by a debt-to-equity ratio of 0.4, indicating a relatively low level of financial leverage. The comprehensive income statement and balance sheet provided a transparent view of the company's financial performance and position, adhering to the Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP).
